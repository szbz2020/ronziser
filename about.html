Statement

I'm invested in the intersection of technology, economics, and environmental concerns. Through my sculptures, I aim to explore the concept of the "commons" - the shared resources that belong to all of us - and the ways in which we can work together to protect and preserve them. My work combines traditional techniques with technological systems, using a variety of mediums such as wood and ceramics to create pieces that challenge our perceptions of ownership and community. My artistic process is ongoing and iterative, continually remaking and evolving my sculptures as a means of exploring new ideas and possibilities. I invite viewers to consider the impact of our actions on the world around us and to explore the vision of a more equitable and sustainable future.

Bio

Ron Ziser is a London-born visual artist with a background in Fine Art and Art History. After completing his undergraduate degree at Goldsmiths University, Ron spent several years running a successful architecture visualization business. During this period Ron used open-access workshops to experiment with different CNC processes and even ran a short-lived pop-up art gallery. He then returned to academia and completed a post-graduate degree in sculpture at the Royal College of Art. Today, Ron continues to create sculptures and images that combine a wide range of disciplines, materials and techniques.
